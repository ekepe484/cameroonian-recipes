Achu Recipe

Achu soup is a traditional food in Cameroon, a yellow soup.It is made with cocoyam.Spices, water, palm oil, and "Canwa / Nikki" (limestone), and fish are other ingredients. It is a common dish originating from the Western and North West province.

Ingredients:

  • Cocoyam (Taro): The main starchy ingredient, pounded or processed into a paste for a thick texture.
  • Palm Oil: Provides the characteristic yellow color and flavor.
  • 700g Meat beef, smoke fish, garden eggs, mushrooms, tripe, cow, stock fish, skin ( All well cooked).
  • 4 cups Water.
  • 1 tsp bouillon powder or maggie.
  • 1 tbsp ground limestone.
  • ½ cup warm red palm oil.
  • 1 hot pepper Habenero.
  • 1/2 tsp Salt to taste.

Steps:

  1. Wash and cut the beef, goat meat, and cow skin into small pieces.
  2. In a large pot, combine the meat, chopped onion, salt, and seasoning cubes.
  3. Add enough water to cover the meat and boil until tender. Keep the stock for the soup.
  4. Add the smoked fish and let it simmer for an additional five minutes.
  5. Dissolve the limestone in ½ cup of warm water and let it sit for a few minutes.
  6. In a separate bowl, mix the palm oil with the dissolved limestone water and stir vigorously until it turns yellow.
  7. Gradually add the hot meat stock while stirring continuously to prevent lumps.
  8. Incorporate the crayfish, onion, bush pepper, nutmeg, garlic, and ginger.
  9. Stir well and adjust salt and seasoning to taste.
  10. Boil the cocoyams with their skins on until soft (about 30 minutes).
  11. Peel the cocoyams while they are still warm.
  12. Pound them using a mortar and pestle, adding a little palm oil and warm water to achieve a smooth texture.
  13. Shape the pounded Achu into a mound on a plate.
  14. Make a well in the center and pour in the warm yellow soup.
  15. Arrange the assorted meats and fish around the soup.
  16. Enjoy your delicious and authentic Cameroonian Achu Soup!
